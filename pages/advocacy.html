Advocacy

Replacing gatekeeping with PLHIV leadership.

MIPA Is a Demand for Power

Positive Change Movement advocates for HIV systems where people living with HIV have real authority, not symbolic seats. MIPA means PLHIV help design programs, direct resources, evaluate outcomes, shape policy, and hold institutions accountable.

Our Advocacy Initiatives

Advocacy is the heartbeat of Positive Change Movement. Our statewide CHW training and certification work is part of that advocacy because it creates a practical pathway for PLHIV to replace gatekeeping with leadership.

PLHIV-Led Decision-Making

Demanding governance, advisory, executive, staff, evaluation, and funding structures where PLHIV hold real authority.

CHW Workforce Power

Building statewide training, certification, fair pay, advancement, and workplace protections for PLHIV Community Health Workers.

HIV Justice

Fighting HIV criminalization, stigma, retaliation, racism, homophobia, transphobia, and policies that harm PLHIV and impacted communities.

Accountability Standards

Challenging organizations that claim to be community-led while excluding PLHIV from power, pay, protection, and decision-making.

Community Education

Promoting U=U, rights education, harm reduction, stigma reduction, and community knowledge led by people with lived experience.

Leadership Pipeline

Preparing PLHIV to serve as trainers, supervisors, board members, policy advocates, public speakers, and systems-change leaders.
