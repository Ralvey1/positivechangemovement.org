Positive Change Movement

Our Mission and Vision

The Positive Change Movement is a community-driven initiative dedicated to championing health, advocacy, and empowerment for individuals living with HIV/AIDS, those affected by HIV/AIDS, and all marginalized communities. We are committed to upholding the principles of Meaningful Involvement of People Living with HIV/AIDS (MIPA) to ensure that their voices are at the forefront of our work.

We envision a world where individuals from all backgrounds, identities, and experiences can thrive free from discrimination, stigma, and barriers to health and equity. Guided by the principles of MIPA, we strive to dismantle systems of oppression, promote inclusivity, and build a just and equitable society.

What CHANGE Means to Us

Championing Health

Bridging gaps in care through peer support, navigation, and programs like our Community Health Worker Program.

Health Equity

Ensuring access to equitable care and addressing systemic health disparities.

Advocacy

Fighting for HIV decriminalization, anti-oppressive policies, and human rights, including reproductive rights and LGBTQIA+ rights.

Nurturing Growth

Providing education, leadership opportunities, and empowerment for PLWHA and all marginalized communities.

Generating Support

Creating peer-led spaces for support, understanding, and resource access.

Ending Stigma

Promoting education and awareness campaigns, such as U=U (Undetectable=Untransmittable), to reduce stigma.
